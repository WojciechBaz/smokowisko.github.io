Virtual Dragon Adoption Program

To sustain the Park’s statutory mission, the Park Directors and our devoted Custodians—the rangers, educators, and researchers who care for Smokowisko every day— have created a way for friends of nature to help directly: the virtual adoption of one of our dragons. Becoming a Patron means your gift supports habitat protection, research, food and veterinary care, and education programs that inspire future guardians.

Patronage has deep roots here. In 1894, Countess Elżbieta Dąbrowska endowed the first “quiet grove” to shield nesting sites. That same year, composer Aleksander Siennicki pledged proceeds from a charity concert series to restore a flight corridor, while explorer Jan Franciszek Orłowski supplied alpine herbs for convalescing drakes. In modern times, organizations such as the Polish Nature Alliance, the Green Future Foundation, and the International Conservation Trust have all contributed to Smokowisko’s patron fund—help that lets us protect dragons where they truly belong: in the wild.

Who Can Become a Patron?

  • Age: 8–100. Younger applicants are welcome with consent of a legal guardian.
  • Spirit: Patrons strive to be brave, kind, curious, and wise—good examples for dragons and people alike.
  • Nature of adoption: Symbolic & virtual. No dragon is relocated; your support funds conservation and research, and you receive updates about your adopted dragon.
