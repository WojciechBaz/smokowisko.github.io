The Foundation and Mission of Smokowisko National Dragon Park

Smokowisko National Dragon Park was founded in 1893 by a coalition of naturalists, folklorists, and local guardians. Among its notable patrons were Count Janusz Radziwiłł, a respected statesman and patron of the sciences; Professor Helena Modrzewska, an early zoologist from Kraków University; and Captain Władysław Brzeziński, a retired cavalry officer known for his deep knowledge of Poland’s wilderness. These historical figures combined their influence, expertise, and resources to ensure the park’s creation and legal protection.

The founders acted in response to accelerating industrial forestry in the foothills and the growing urgency to preserve the last breeding populations of Polish Forest Dragons. Early caretakers documented nesting sites, mapped feeding glades, and negotiated seasonal forest quiet hours with local villages—customs that became the backbone of the Park’s management traditions.

In its early years (1893–1914), the park operated informally under local statutes and civic patronage. Rangers traveled on foot between scattered shelters, often guided by oral reports from hunters and herbalists. The now-legendary Green Lantern Station hosted the first longitudinal field notes on dragon migration corridors and molting cycles.

The Park’s formalization in the interwar period brought the first comprehensive charter and a clear legal mandate for habitat protection. This included the creation of a ring of buffer groves to reduce disturbance near roosting crags and the establishment of the Smokowisko Custodians—a council balancing conservation with cultural heritage.

Today, only certain designated areas of the park are open to tourists. Visitors may explore safe observation points, guided trails, and educational exhibits. For safety and conservation reasons, the core forest is off-limits to the public due to the potential of encountering wild dragons. Only trained park rangers may enter these protected areas. Nonetheless, many visitors report thrilling glimpses of dragons flying overhead or moving through the trees.

The current dedicated staff includes Head Ranger Piotr Kowalski, Conservation Biologist Dr. Marta Zielińska, Education Coordinator Aneta Nowak, and Senior Caretaker Tomasz Wróbel. Together, they carry forward the founders’ mission of habitat protection, research, and public stewardship.
