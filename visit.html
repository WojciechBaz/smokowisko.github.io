Plan Your Visit

Smokowisko is a vast mountain–forest reserve with views that steal your breath—and residents who deserve your respect. Below you’ll find trails, viewpoints, lodging, camping, events and safety guidance.

General information

Area: approx. 38,400 ha (core + buffer). The core holds old‑growth mixed forest, limestone cliffs and river wetlands.

Visitor Center: Forest Meadow (parking, tickets, exhibit, restrooms, small eatery).

Note: the core zone is ranger‑only. Visitors may use designated trails and viewpoints.

Dragons & visiting
  • The Park does not guarantee a dragon sighting.
  • Very often you can hear calls at dawn or notice signs (shed scales, tracks, soot on ledges).
  • Best distant view: Dragon Ledge viewpoint — silhouettes are sometimes visible at dusk over the ridges.

Scenic Trails & Viewpoints

TrailDistance / timeDifficultyHighlights along the way
1. Golden Brooks Valley 7.5 km • 2–3 h (loop) Easy Meandering river with green lichens, boardwalks over wetlands, Echo Spring (natural reverb like dragon whistles), kingfisher banks.
2. Dragon Ledge (viewpoint) 4.2 km • 1.5 h round‑trip Easy/Moderate Balcony on a limestone cliff; panorama of flight corridors. Dusk silhouettes of Draconis sylvanus are sometimes seen.
3. Green Gully Loop 12 km • 4–5 h Moderate/Hard Limestone ravines, the “Organ Slabs” (wind makes a low tone), “Lantern” vista towards the core zone.
4. Mill Spires 9 km • 3–4 h Moderate Basalt columns, star‑moss meadow, Curtain Falls (best after rain).
5. Quiet Mornings Path 5 km • 1.5–2 h Easy Reedbed platforms; at first light a golden haze from D. aureomista displays may be visible.

All trails run alongside the core boundary—please stay on marked paths and platforms.

Lodging & Camping

Where to stay

  • Green Lantern Lodge – 32 beds, self‑catering kitchen, drying room; 1.2 km from the Visitor Center.
  • Dragon Ledge Cabins – 6 cabins (2–4 guests), quiet‑hours zone.
  • Forest Meadow Guesthouse – rooms next to the Visitor Center (weekends fill fast).

Campgrounds

  • Iskra Meadow – 20 pitches, fire ring only; firewood available on site.
  • Brzezina Riverside – 12 pitches by the river (filter your water!), peaceful at dawn.

Camping is allowed only at designated campgrounds and with a permit. No entry to the core zone at dusk or night.

Seasonal Closures & Safety

  • Nesting season: 15 April – 31 July — closed: upper parts of Green Gully Loop; approach to the Organ Slabs. Dragons can be more territorial.
  • Hibernation: December – February — limited access to Dragon Ledge (dark‑sky quiet hours).
  • Weather: after storms, boardwalks in Golden Brooks Valley may close temporarily.
Rules: stay on trail, keep distance from cliff edges, no drones, no food left behind. If you spot a dragon—admire from afar, do not interact, and report the sighting at the Visitor Center.

Food & Practical Info

Where to eat

  • Latarnia Café – coffee, honey‑lime tea, grab‑and‑go sandwiches.
  • Brzezina Kiosk – seasonal, by the riverside camp.
